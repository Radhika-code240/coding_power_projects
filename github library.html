The rare book section of a library is a unique and invaluable space, man, first editions. These books are typically preserved with great care, as they may be fragile, irreplaceable, or rare in their content and form.Scholars, researchers, and bibliophiles visit these sections to access primary sources, gain insight into historical contexts, or study the evolution of literature and printing techniques.
Libraries organize collections into sections based on subjects for efficient navigation and research. The main sections include reference, circulation and periodicals, housing encyclopedias, fiction and non-fiction works, and newspapers.Subject-based sections cater to specific disciplines: Arts and Humanities (art, music, literature), Social Sciences (history, psychology), Science and Technology (physics, chemistry, biology), Business and Economics (management, finance), and Health and Medicine (medical sciences, nursing).
The Newspaper Section in a library offers access to current and past issues of local, national and international newspapers. This section serves as a valuable resource for research, projects and assignments, providing current awareness on latest news, events and trends.The collection typically includes national, local and regional newspapers, as well as international publications. Many libraries also provide online newspaper databases and microfilm/microfiche archives for historical research. The section offers a quiet reading area for focused study.

The Constitution outlines the structure of government, defines the powers of the executive, legislature, and judiciary, and protects the rights of citizens through **Fundamental Rights* (Part III). Rules And Regulations


1. All students and teaching staff are members of the college library.

2. The library shall be functioning on all working days from 9.00 A.M to 5.00 P.M.

3. Silence shall strictly be observed in all parts of the Library.

4. Entrance to the Reading Room and the Reference Section is permitted only to students who have their identity cards with them.

5. The Librarian has the right to recall a book at 24 hours' notice.

6. The Library is divided into The Reference Library and Reading Room.
